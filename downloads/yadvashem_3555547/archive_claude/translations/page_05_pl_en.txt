Testimony text

I was born in Borysław in 1908. My father was for a period of 35 years an official in an oil mine belonging to the "Panto" company. He was a very believing man, thoroughly traditional. Hence in our family we observed the customs of Jewish tradition without being devout, however. I was the eldest in the family and when father was called to military service I began earning work to support the family. I was in a group of Jewish youth which already in this period engaged in sports in order to develop physical fitness and hardiness in ourselves. By nature I was energetic and active, therefore I quickly moved to the front of this group. My colleagues were: Israel Haas /currently in Israel/, Adam Raj /also residing in Israel/, Eliasz Riezeles /currently in Poland/, Filip Liebessman and Hermann Leinor /deceased/. With the passage of time our youth group moved to the front of the then young generation in terms of combativeness, activity and sporting fitness. The approaching period showed how important these traits became necessary and what role they played in our activities. In the first years after World War I we did not yet feel in Borysław any kind of organized anti-Semitic action.
