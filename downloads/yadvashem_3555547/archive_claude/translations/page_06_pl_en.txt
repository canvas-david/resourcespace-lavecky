This can be attributed to the fact that the majority of Jews worked in large oil enterprises, wax companies and so forth. Though there were also many craftsmen, small merchants, transport workers and others. The merchant class was also quite numerous. When organized anti-Semitic movement began - the attitude of these various groups was generally solidary, but limited itself to so-called passive resistance. Protection was sought primarily through legal channels from administrative and police authorities. Influence was utilized in various Polish circles to halt anti-Jewish actions, naturally with varying degrees of success. As far as I remember well, until Piłsudski's death in 1935 such interventions often achieved immediate successes. I remember that after Piłsudski's death many Jews in Borysław wept sincerely. For they tied their fate and the stability of their situation to Piłsudski's person. The creation of the Camp of National Unity, thus in a way official patronage of anti-Semitic action, considerably revived the nationalist elements of Borysław. At the head of this action stood people from the "Strzelec" organizations, Sokół organizations and so forth. Various thugs and hooligans naturally joined them as well. Facts of Jews being beaten by gangs composed precisely of the aforementioned elements began to occur more and more frequently. Economic boycott action began on a broader scale, picketing of Jewish shops. Nationalist elements began to feel stronger in the center, also occupying important positions among the ruling factors. Hence the previous forms of Jewish defense became insufficient. Therefore, then partly on my initiative, a group of our youth which I already mentioned began to actively react to persecutions in an organized manner. Namely, if it came to our knowledge that in such and such a place they were either to picket Jewish shops or simply attack Jews - we undertook retaliatory action. There were several dozen of us young, energetic and sympathetic people, but above all determined to provide help to those being attacked.
