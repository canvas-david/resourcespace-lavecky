Text of testimony
I was born in Borysław in 1908. My father was for a period
of 35 years a clerk at an oil mine belonging to the "Fanto" company.
He was a man half-believing, rather traditional. Hence in
our family we observed the customs of Jewish tradition
without however being devout. I was the eldest in the family and
when father was called up for military service I began gainful work
to support the family. I was in a group of Jewish youth
which already at that time engaged in sports in order to develop
physical fitness and resilience. By nature I was energetic and
active, therefore I quickly rose to the forefront of this group.
My colleagues were: Izrael Haas /currently in Israel/, Adas Haj
/also residing in Israel/, Eliasz Riszeles /currently in Poland/,
Filip Liebesman and Herman Leiner /deceased/. With the passage of time
our youth group rose to the forefront of the young generation
of that time in terms of combativeness, activity and athletic
fitness. The coming period showed how precisely these qualities became
necessary and what role they played in our activities. In the first
years after World War I we did not yet feel in
Borysław any organized anti-Semitic action. In the years
1925/6 anti-Jewish movements already began, however only
